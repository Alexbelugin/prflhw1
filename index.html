Common questions about sleep optimization

How is this different from generic sleep apps?
We focus specifically on remote work challenges: inconsistent schedules, excessive screen time, home office lighting, and async collaboration fatigue. Our programs address the unique circadian disruptions that come from working where you sleep, with interventions designed for distributed teams rather than individuals.
What data do you collect and how is it protected?
We collect sleep quality metrics through validated questionnaires (Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index, Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire), never through invasive tracking. All data is encrypted, GDPR-compliant, and aggregated for team reports. Individual data is only visible to the employee unless explicitly shared.
How long are the workshops and when do they happen?
Group workshops run 45-60 minutes and are scheduled across multiple time zones to ensure everyone can attend during their optimal learning window (typically mid-morning). Sessions are recorded for async viewing, with interactive components adapted for self-paced completion.
Can we implement this gradually with just one team first?
Absolutely. Many clients start with a pilot team of 10-25 people to measure impact before company-wide rollout. We'll help you select an appropriate pilot group and establish success metrics. Pilot programs typically run 8-12 weeks with clear go/no-go decision points.
What if employees are skeptical about "workplace sleep coaching"?
We frame everything as performance optimization, not medical intervention. Participation is always voluntary, and we emphasize personal benefits like more energy for hobbies and family time. Our coaches are trained to work with skeptics by focusing on small, practical wins rather than dramatic lifestyle changes.
How do you measure success beyond self-reported metrics?
While we prioritize subjective wellbeing, we also track objective indicators like meeting attendance patterns, async response times, and voluntary overtime hours. Some clients integrate with their HRIS to monitor sick day usage and performance review scores, though this is entirely optional.
Do you provide equipment like blue-light glasses or smart bulbs?
Our Growth and Scale plans include recommended equipment lists with vetted suppliers and bulk discount codes. While we don't directly provide hardware, we guide purchasing decisions and can coordinate drop-shipping for larger teams. The focus remains on behavioral change and environmental design rather than gadgets.
What happens after the initial program period?
Most teams transition to a maintenance phase with monthly check-ins, quarterly workshops for new hires, and annual program refreshers. We provide self-service resources and train internal "sleep champions" to sustain momentum. Continuous improvement is tracked through bi-annual assessments.
